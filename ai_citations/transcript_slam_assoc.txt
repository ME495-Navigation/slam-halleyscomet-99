Date: 2026-03-21
Topic: EKF SLAM data association — Mahalanobis vs Euclidean distance

--- Summary of interaction ---

I implemented Mahalanobis distance gating for data association as
described in Thrun, Burgard, Fox — Probabilistic Robotics Ch. 10, but
the EKF was diverging: newly detected landmarks were being incorrectly
associated with existing ones immediately after initialisation.

I asked why the Mahalanobis distance would produce near-zero values for
all incoming measurements when a landmark had just been added to the state.

The explanation was: newly initialised landmarks are assigned a large
initial covariance (1e6), which makes the innovation covariance S very
large, so S^-1 is near zero, and the Mahalanobis distance
  d = nu^T * S^-1 * nu
collapses to near zero for *every* measurement.  This causes all
measurements to appear to match the new landmark regardless of their
actual position.

I asked what a practical alternative was that avoids this degenerate case.
The suggested approach was to project the measurement into the map frame
using the current pose estimate and use Euclidean distance directly:
  mx_pred = xi(0) + r * cos(phi + xi(2))
  my_pred = xi(1) + r * sin(phi + xi(2))
and gate on ||[mx_pred, my_pred] - [lm_x, lm_y]|| < threshold.

This is less statistically rigorous but stable at initialisation.
