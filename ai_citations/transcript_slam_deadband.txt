Topic: Spurious SLAM drift when robot is stationary on physical hardware

--- Summary of interaction ---

On the real TurtleBot3, the green robot (SLAM estimate) was accumulating
position drift even when the robot was not moving.  The path was building
up small random offsets at ~100 Hz.

I asked what could cause the EKF prediction step to receive non-zero
wheel deltas when the motors were off.

The explanation was that physical encoder hardware produces low-level
quantisation noise: each joint_states message reports a slightly different
position even when the wheels are stationary, because the encoder
resolution is finite and the firmware samples it continuously.  Feeding
these micro-deltas into the diff-drive kinematics and EKF prediction
accumulates a random walk.

I asked what the standard approach is to filter this out without
completely disabling the prediction step.  The suggested approach was a
dead-band on the delta magnitudes:
  if |dl| < DEADBAND && |dr| < DEADBAND: return early

with DEADBAND = 0.01 rad, which is well below any intentional motion
(typical cmd_vel produces deltas ~0.1 rad per callback) but above the
encoder noise floor on this hardware.
