Topic: Pratt circle fitting — incorrect eigenvalue selection in fit_circle()

--- Summary of interaction ---

I was implementing the Pratt algebraic circle fitting algorithm and the
fitted circle centres were consistently offset from the true obstacle
positions in simulation.  I asked why selecting the largest eigenvalue
of the Q matrix (formed as Y * H_inv * Y) produced wrong results, and
whether the correct solution requires the smallest or largest eigenvalue.

The key insight provided was: Eigen::SelfAdjointEigenSolver returns
eigenvalues in *ascending* order (smallest first), and the Pratt method
requires the eigenvector corresponding to the smallest *positive*
eigenvalue — not the largest.  My original loop was finding the maximum,
which is incorrect.

I then asked how to safely skip near-zero or negative eigenvalues that
arise from numerical noise, and the suggested guard was:
  if (eigenvalues(i) > 1e-12 && eigenvalues(i) < min_val)

This fix resolved the systematic position error in simulation.
